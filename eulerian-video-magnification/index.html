Final Project: Eulerian Video Magnification by Thien Le and Wentinn Liao

Part 1. Laplacian Pyramid

We construct the Laplacian pyramid by repeatedly downsampling each frame of the video, then rescaling all the downsampled frames to the original size. Because both downsampling ignores the channel dimension, we merge the time dimension with the channel dimension to allow clean vectorization, and restore the time dimension afterwards.

Part 2. Temporal Filtering

To magnify the specific temporal frequencies, we perform a 1D Fourier transform on the time dimension. We then index the frequencies with a magnitude in the range we want, and scale them by a factor, chosen to be 3. Finally, we transform back and collapse the stack. The process is vectorized by collapsing the Laplacian stack dimension and the entire image dimension into a single batch dimension, which is then restored after the Fourier transform back.
